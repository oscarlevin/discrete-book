In my safe is a sheet of paper with two shapes drawn on it in colored crayon. One is a triangle, and the other is a diamond. Each shape is drawn in a single color. Suppose you believe me when I tell you that,
if the triangle is purple, then the diamond is orange.
What do you therefore know about the truth value of the following statements?
  1. If the diamond is not orange, then the triangle is not purple.
  2. The triangle and the diamond are both purple.
  3. If the diamond is orange, then the triangle is purple.
  4. The triangle the diamond are both orange.
  5. The triangle is not purple or the diamond is orange.
