Definition 1.2.6. Converse, Contrapositive, and Inverse.

Given an implication \(P \imp Q\text{,}\) we say,
  • Theconverseisthe statement\(Q \imp P\text{.}\)
  • Thecontrapositiveis the statement\(\neg Q \imp \neg P\text{.}\)
  • The inverse is the statement, \(\neg P \imp \neg Q\text{.}\)
